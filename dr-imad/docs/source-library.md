## NEVER Use as Sources

- Wikipedia (use for starting research only, never cite)
- Random health blogs (Healthline OK if Tier 2 backup, never alone)
- Pinterest, Quora, Reddit
- Affiliate health sites
- Pirated journal uploads (ResearchGate paywalled paper PDFs)
- Patient testimonial websites
- Alternative medicine sites (Ayurveda alone, naturopathy claims)
- Anti-vaccine sites
- Sites making "miracle cure" claims
- Hospital marketing pages claiming superiority without data

For inline citations:
> "...peritoneal cancer 5-year survival rates following CRS+HIPEC range from 30-50% in selected patients (NCCN Guidelines, 2023)."

For footer citations (where multiple sources used):
```
Sources:
1. NCCN Clinical Practice Guidelines in Oncology — Peritoneal Carcinomatosis (2023)
2. WHO Global Cancer Observatory (GLOBOCAN 2020)
3. Sugarbaker PH et al. Cytoreductive surgery and HIPEC. Lancet Oncol. 2018;19(7):e376-e384.
```

## Stat Verification Checklist

Before stating any number on the website:
- [ ] Is this stat from Tier 1, 2, or 3 source?
- [ ] Year of publication checked (current within 5 years preferred)?
- [ ] Indian context applicable, or US/EU stat being misapplied?
- [ ] Range cited (not single number) where relevant?
- [ ] Source attributed inline or in footnote?
- [ ] Not from a hospital marketing page presenting unaudited claims?
